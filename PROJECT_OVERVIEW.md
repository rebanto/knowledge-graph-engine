# Product Overview

A research tool that answers questions using **only the documents you give it** —
and proves every answer against them. Instead of reading your documents as plain
text the way a normal chatbot does, it builds a map of how everything in them
connects — people, organizations, papers, ideas, and the links between them — then
answers by following that map. Every answer comes with its sources and a **trust
score** showing how much of it is actually backed by your material.

---

## What it does

- **Answers from your own sources.** Add research papers, PDFs, web pages, or news
  feeds. Every fact links back to where it came from.
- **Maps how things connect**, so it can answer relationship questions a normal
  chatbot can't — like "how is this researcher connected to that idea?" — and show
  the chain step by step.
- **Flags disagreements.** When two of your sources contradict each other, it says
  so instead of quietly picking one.
- **Scores its own answers.** A built-in fact-checker reviews each answer and
  reports what share of it is backed by your sources, listing anything it couldn't
  verify. In the web app this appears as a **claim ledger**: the answer's factual
  claims are marked supported or unsupported.
- **Deep Research mode** for hard questions: it works like a small research team —
  splits the question into parts, researches each, combines the findings, and
  fact-checks the result.
- **A research cockpit on every workspace.** Users see source readiness, corpus
  mix, saved threads, graph availability, and high-value research moves before
  they ask.
- **Reusable reports.** Answers include an evidence strip, conflict signal,
  copy-to-clipboard, and Markdown export so findings can leave the app intact.
- **Works as memory for other AI tools.** Other assistants (like Claude or Cursor)
  can pull verified, connected facts from it instead of guessing.
- **Plus the basics done well:** a visual map you can explore, natural follow-up
  questions, saved answers, separate projects for different topics, and it works on
  any subject.

**The features that set it apart, in one line each:**

- **Maps how things connect** — most tools (NotebookLM, Perplexity, ChatGPT,
  Elicit) just read text and can't see relationships. Only this product, GraphRAG,
  and Glean build a real map; scite has a narrow version (links between papers).
- **Traces multi-step connections** — following a trail like "A worked with B, who
  cited C, which contradicts D." Practically none of the others do this; it's
  something a normal chatbot simply can't.
- **Flags disagreements** — only this product surfaces contradictions in your own
  sources. scite has a citation-based version; the rest hide them.
- **Trust score** — an independent fact-check on every answer, shown as a number.
  **No other tool on this list does this.**
- **Deep Research** — Perplexity and ChatGPT have "deep research" modes, but over
  the open web and without a self-check; this one runs a research team over *your*
  sources and verifies the result.
- **Memory for other AI tools** — letting other assistants pull verified facts from
  it. **Unique on this list.**

**Where the others are genuinely better:** Perplexity searches the entire live web;
NotebookLM can turn your documents into a podcast-style audio summary; ChatGPT and
Claude are better all-purpose assistants for writing and brainstorming. This
product is a specialist for trustworthy research, not a do-everything chatbot.

## What only this product does (its edge)

No other tool combines these:

1. **A trust score on every answer** — it fact-checks itself and shows the number.
2. **Flags contradictions** between your own sources instead of hiding them.
3. **Traces multi-step connections** through a real map of your material.
4. **A self-checking research team** for hard questions, working over your sources.
5. **Can be the verified memory** that other AI tools plug into.

> **In one line:** it understands how your sources connect, proves every answer
> against them with a visible trust score, and can even be the reliable memory
> other AI tools are missing.
